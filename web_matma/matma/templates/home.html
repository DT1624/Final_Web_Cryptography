Digital Signature

A Digital Signature is a cryptographic technique used to validate the authenticity and integrity of a message or document. It ensures that the content has not been altered in transit and that the sender's identity can be verified.

Why Digital Signatures Matter

Digital signatures are the cornerstone of modern electronic communication and transactions. They use asymmetric encryption methods like RSA and ECC to securely sign documents, ensuring that the sender cannot deny their involvement in the transaction. This provides both security and legal verification.

Digital signatures are widely used in securing email communications, software distribution, and online transactions. They offer non-repudiation, meaning that once a message or document is signed, the sender cannot deny its authenticity, thus fostering trust and accountability in digital interactions.
